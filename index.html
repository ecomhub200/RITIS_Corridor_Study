🎯 How the priority score works

Each corridor gets an OCRA combined score — higher means retime sooner. It multiplies how bad delay is, how unreliable it is, how much traffic it carries, and how long since it was last retimed.

Norm Delaymedian delay per signal × Norm IQRday-to-day swing per signal × √(AADT/1000)traffic volume × √Tyears since retimed

📦 Where the data comes from

The tool ranks 32 corridors across Henrico County, VA, built from 619 INRIX XD probe segments.

Source & freshness

Travel-time readings come from INRIX XD probe data via RITIS, in 15-minute intervals. The reference (free-flow) speed for each segment is the 25th percentile of its night-time travel times. The freshness line on the Upload page shows the exact window loaded.

Your privacy

Everything runs entirely in your browser. The files are read locally and decompressed on your machine — no data ever leaves your computer, and there is no server doing the math.

💬 Frequently asked

Why do the analysis views look locked?

They unlock as soon as you load and Process data. Until then only Upload Data is active.

Why is the OCRA table showing a yellow warning?

Because AADT and years-since-retimed are still placeholders. Enter the real values in the inputs and press Recalculate to clear it.

What does "delay per signal" mean?

The median extra travel time over free-flow, pooled across both directions and divided by the corridor's signal count — so corridors of different sizes compare fairly.

Why are there two directions per road?

Each road is analyzed eastbound/westbound (or north/south) separately, since congestion is often directional. Network totals de-duplicate signals and miles so a road isn't counted twice.

How do I refresh the data?

Re-run the prepared dataset, or upload a newer RITIS Massive Data Downloader export (INRIX XD, 15-min, with travel time) plus the XD identification file on the Upload page.

How the calculation works
  1. Corridor travel time — segment travel times are summed per 15-minute interval (scaled if some segments lack data).
  2. Free-flow reference — 25th percentile of each segment’s night-time travel time, summed over the corridor (the "All Nights 25th % Night" file); INRIX reference speed as fallback.
  3. Delay — actual minus free-flow, for the selected weekdays during AM / Mid-day / PM.
  4. Normalize — median delay (sec) and delay IQR (min), pooled over both directions and divided by the number of signals.
  5. Score — NormDelay × NormIQR × √(AADT ÷ 1,000) × √T, exactly the OCRA spreadsheet formula.
  6. Speed percentiles — corridor space-mean speeds (miles ÷ travel time) per 15-min interval; 15th/50th/85th/95th percentiles for screening and before/after comparison. Per-segment percentiles available as a CSV download.
